Ingredients

  • Steak; Any Cut (We Reccomend Ribeye)
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Oil
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Take the steak out of the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes. This ensures even cooking.
  2. Generously season both sides of the steak with salt and black pepper. The seasoning will enhance the natural flavors of the meat.
  3. Heat a heavy-bottomed skillet or cast-iron pan over high heat. Add a drizzle of oil, enough to coat the bottom of the pan, and wait until it starts to shimmer.
  4. Carefully place the seasoned steak in the hot pan. Cook it undisturbed for a few minutes on each side, depending on the thickness and desired doneness. For a medium-rare steak, aim for about 3-4 minutes per side.
  5. Once the steak is cooked to your liking, you can add a pat of butter to the pan and let it melt over the steak. Tilt the pan slightly and use a spoon to baste the steak with the melted butter, adding extra flavor and richness.
  6. Remove the steak from the pan and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a tender and juicy result. Enjoy your perfectly pan-seared steak with your favorite sides!
